Infected Insect Bites

(For adults and children aged 1 year and over) [cite: 111]
Exclude: pregnant individuals under 16 years [cite: 112]

Consider the risk of deterioration, red flags or serious illness[cite: 117, 118]:
  • Signs of systemic hypersensitivity reaction or anaphylaxis [cite: 121, 123]
  • Severely immunosuppressed and have signs/symptoms of an infection [cite: 122, 125]
  • Stings with risk of airway obstruction or concerns of orbital cellulitis [cite: 126]
Gateway Point
Administer adrenaline (if anaphylaxis) [cite: 124]

Consider calculating NEWS2 Score ahead of signposting patient to A&E or calling 999[cite: 129].
Does the patient meet ANY of the following criteria? [cite: 130]
  • Bite or scratch caused by animal(s) or human(s) [cite: 131, 132]
  • Bite by tick in the UK and signs of Lyme disease (erythema migrans) [cite: 135]
  • Bite/sting while travelling outside UK (concern of malaria, tick borne encephalitis) [cite: 136]
  • Bite or sting caused by an unusual or exotic insect [cite: 137]

• Other provider [cite: 146, 147]
Has it been at least 48 hours after the initial insect bite or sting? [cite: 139]
Is itch the principal symptom? [cite: 141]
(In the absence of other signs of infection) [cite: 143]
Skin redness and itching are common (lasts up to 10 days). Avoiding scratching reduces infection risk[cite: 181, 184, 190, 193].

Recommend self care, oral antihistamine and/or topical steroids over the counter[cite: 148].
Infected insect bite less likely[cite: 150, 154].

Recommend self care, oral antihistamine and/or topical steroids over the counter[cite: 167].
Gateway Point
Does the patient have acute onset of ≥3 of the following symptoms of an infected insect bite? [cite: 151, 152]
  • Redness of skin [cite: 151]
  • Pain or tenderness to the area [cite: 154]
  • Swelling of skin [cite: 155]
  • Skin surrounding the bite feels hot to touch [cite: 158]
Redness and swelling of skin surrounding the bite is spreading.

Clearly demarcate the area and ask patient to monitor[cite: 160, 161].

Recommend self care, oral antihistamine and/or topical steroids[cite: 168, 169]. Return if worsens or no improvement in 3 days[cite: 163, 164, 165].
Does the patient meet ANY of the following criteria: [cite: 171]
  • Patient systemically unwell [cite: 172]
  • Known comorbidity which may complicate (e.g. peripheral arterial disease, chronic venous insufficiency, lymphoedema or morbid obesity) [cite: 176, 177]
  • Severe pain out of proportion to the wound [cite: 178]
  • Patient has significant collection of fluid or pus [cite: 178]
